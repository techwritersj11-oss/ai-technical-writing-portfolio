## Overview

This guide is designed for users who are new to working with AI systems such as chatbots, assistants, or generative tools.

It explains:
- How to write effective prompts
- Common mistakes to avoid
- How to improve AI responses through iteration
- What AI can and cannot reliably do

The goal is to help users communicate clearly with AI systems and get useful, trustworthy results.

---

## What Is a Prompt?

A **prompt** is the instruction or question you give an AI system.

Good prompts provide:
- Context
- Clear intent
- Specific expectations

Poor prompts are vague, incomplete, or assume the AI understands unstated details.

## When AI Works Best

AI performs well for:
- Drafting content
- Rewriting for clarity
- Summarizing information
- Generating examples
- Improving tone and structure

AI is less reliable for:
- Making factual claims without sources
- Diagnosing system failures
- Explaining undocumented features

---

## Final Tip

Think of prompting as **clear communication**, not programming.

The better you explain what you need, the better the AI can help.
